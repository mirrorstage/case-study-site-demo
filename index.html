Background

A brief round-up of relevant context and information about the project environment: the market, organization, or other high-level facts. State the nature of the problem or goal that made the project necessary.

Starting Point

Provide details about the situation as found at the start the project. What already existed that either was causing the issue or had failed to resolve it? Consider using visuals to illustrate or demonstrate. This is especially useful if you're changing or replacing something that already existed, rather than starting fresh.

Discovery

Your approach to addressing even an ostensibly well-understood problem will benefit from a period of exploration or research. How did you make sure the the problem that appeared was actually what needed to be solved? How did you validate or evolve your understanding of it? What techniques or frameworks did you find beneficial for digging deeper? Detail your approaches and your conclusions, and then tie your enhanced understanding to your proposed next steps.

This is also a point at which your process has led you to form hypotheses about the intended results of any interventions you propose. State them here as well.

Delivery

This section can be two sub-sections: first design, then implemenetation. Both are stages that are typically rich in visual artifacts, so be sure to include what you can.

Design

In the first sub-section, relate both the details of your chosen intervention and also any constraints that guided you along the way. Perhaps you were operating within a certain time frame or budget. Perhaps there were limits to how deep into the business model or organizational context your solution could reach. When you're designing a solution, you must always also prioritize. What did you leave out or leave for later?

Implementation

In this stage, you're reaching the point where you bring the solution to readiness and then online (for whatever value of "online" makes sense in your milieu). Nobody does alone, so this sub-section is a good point to include the contributions of others on your team to the work.

Outcomes

After all of the above, what happened? To what extent did the solution succeed for the business, the customers, the users? How did you measure it? What next steps did this stage suggest?
